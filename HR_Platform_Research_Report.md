## COMPANY CONTEXT

**Company Type:** Multi-state field service company (radon mitigation, electrical, waterproofing, coatings)

**Key HR Challenges:**
- Multi-state compliance (different wage laws, tax rules, certifications)

- Vehicle/equipment assignment per employee

## 1.1 PAYCOM

**Overview:** Oklahoma-based, enterprise-grade single-database HRIS. Known for its "Beti" self-service payroll model where employees verify their own pay before processing. Targets mid-to-large companies (50–10,000+ employees). Single unified database across all modules — no integrations between modules needed.

### Core Navigation / Module Structure

**Main Menu Sections:**
- Talent Acquisition
- Time and Labor Management
- Payroll
- Talent Management
- HR Management
- Benefits Administration
- Employee Self-Service (ESS)
- Manager Self-Service (MSS)

---

### A. HRIS / Employee Data (Paycom)

**Employee Record Sub-Tabs:**
- Personal Information (legal name, SSN, DOB, address, emergency contacts)
- Employment Information (hire date, department, job title, cost center, FLSA status, EEO class)
- Compensation (base pay, pay type: hourly/salary/piece-rate, pay frequency, multiple pay rates)
- Position History (job changes, promotions, transfers)
- Custom Fields (configurable per company)
- Documents (e-signature, upload, categorized storage)
- Notes / HR Alerts
- Org Chart Position

**Key Data Fields Tracked:**
- Employee ID, SSN, legal name, preferred name

### C. TIME & ATTENDANCE (BambooHR)

**Status:** Add-on module ("Time Tracking")

**Clock-In Methods:**

**Features:**

- Overtime calculation
- Break tracking
- Regular/overtime hours auto-calculation
- GPS location at punch (on mobile)
- Manager timesheet review and approval
- Integration with payroll (auto-sends approved hours)

**Notable Limitation:** Scheduling is basic. BambooHR does not have a robust shift scheduling tool — this is a known gap. Many field service companies integrate with a separate scheduling tool.

- Some states have no income tax (TX, FL, WA, WY, SD, NV, etc.)
- Local tax in cities (NYC, Philadelphia, Cincinnati, Columbus, etc.)
- Reciprocal agreements between states

**State-Specific Wage/Hour Laws (Critical for Field Service):**
- **California:** Daily overtime (>8 hrs/day), 7th-day overtime, 2 mandatory rest breaks + meal period, split-shift premium, waiting time penalty on termination
- **New York:** Spread of hours premium, mandatory written wage notice
- **Washington:** Paid sick leave mandate, predictive scheduling
- **Colorado:** Colorado HELP (4-day work week option), PFML
- **Massachusetts:** Sunday/holiday premium pay
- **Illinois:** WARN Act, Chicago minimum wage
- **Ohio / Pennsylvania / Indiana:** Various local municipality taxes

**Key Compliance Modules:**
- State new hire reporting (first-time filing in each state within 20 days)
- State withholding form collection (varies by state — not all use W-4)
- Workers' comp class code assignment per job
- OSHA 300 log (required for companies with 10+ employees)
- FMLA tracking (required at 50+ employees nationwide OR state FMLA at lower thresholds)
- State-specific PFML (Paid Family Medical Leave)

**License/Certification Compliance (Field Service Specific):**
- State contractor license numbers
- Electrical license (by state, with expiration)
- Radon Mitigation Professional certification (NRPP/NRSB — state-specific)
- Waterproofing certifications
- DOT/CDL requirements (if commercial vehicles over 26,000 GVWR)
- OSHA 10 / OSHA 30 cards
- First aid / CPR certification
- Drug test compliance (pre-employment, random, post-accident)

# SECTION 5: FEATURE GAP ANALYSIS FOR SAFEGUARD FIELD SERVICE

## Features All Major Platforms Handle Well
- Basic employee records
- Multi-state payroll
- Direct deposit
- PTO management
- Basic onboarding (I-9, W-4)
- Standard reporting

## Features Where Gaps Exist for Field Services

### Gap 1: Certification / License Tracking
**Problem:** Technicians need active state licenses (electrical, radon, etc.). None of the SMB platforms have a first-class certification tracking module with:
- Expiration alerts by state
- Required-for-dispatch logic (prevent scheduling uncertified tech)
- Auto-renewal reminders to employee + manager
- Document upload for license scan

**Best Available:** ADP Workforce Now and Workday have basic certification fields. Most platforms require custom fields workaround.

### Gap 2: Vehicle / Equipment Per-Employee Tracking
**Problem:** Each technician is assigned a van with specific equipment. Need to track:
- Vehicle assignment (VIN, plate, state reg)
- Equipment in van (inventory list)
- Mileage tracking
- Vehicle inspection compliance
- GPS telematics integration

**Best Available:** Rippling (device/asset tracking) and BambooHR (asset tab) are closest, but neither handles vehicle telematics or service-specific equipment manifests.

### Gap 3: Job-Site Time Tracking with Revenue Attribution
**Problem:** Field service needs to know:
- Time technician spent on Job A vs. Job B
- Clock-in/out at customer address (geo-verified)
- Connect time records to work orders (revenue)

**Best Available:** Paycom's job-costing punch and ADP's labor distribution come close, but none natively connect to dispatch/CRM systems.

### Gap 4: Commission + Hourly in Same Payroll
**Problem:** Sales reps earn commission; technicians earn hourly + potential bonus. Complex commission rules (tiered, product-based, residual).

**Best Available:** Paycom, ADP, and Paycor handle this well with proper setup. Gusto and BambooHR struggle with complex commission rules.

### Gap 5: Multi-State Workers' Comp by Job/Crew
**Problem:** A tech licensed in OH may work a job in PA — which state's workers' comp applies? Which class code?

**Best Available:** ADP Workforce Now has the most sophisticated workers' comp code assignment, including per-state and per-job-code logic.

### Gap 6: Dispatch / Schedule Integration
**Problem:** HR scheduling and dispatch scheduling are two different things. HR platforms don't talk to ServiceTitan, Jobber, or dispatch boards.

**Best Available:** None natively. Requires custom integration. Rippling's workflow engine is closest to bridging this.

### Gap 7: Safety and OSHA Tracking
**Problem:** Field technicians face real occupational hazards. Need:
- OSHA 300 log
- Near-miss reporting
- Safety training completion tracking
- Workers' comp claim workflow

**Best Available:** ADP has OSHA 300 log. All platforms track training completion. None have a native safety incident reporting module — typically requires integration with a dedicated safety tool.
